20.1 Bohr's theory of Hydrogen atom is based upon several assumptions. Do any of these assumptions contradict classical physics?

For constant potential in a region, the electric field is zero, as,
  • V1 = V2
  • ΔV = V2 - V1 ⇒ ΔV = 0
  • Now, as V = Ed ⇒ E = V/d
  • E = 0
Hence, when potential difference is zero, electric field is also zero.
