13.4 Why does the resistance of a conductor rise with temperature?

  1. with similar and equal charges: In this case, both plates will attract or repel, depending on the charge, with force of same magnitude but opposite direction.
    • F1 + F2 = F - F = 0
    Hence net. force will be zero.
  2. with opposite and equal charges: In this case, both forces are equal and in same direction.
    • F1 + F2 = F + F = 2F
    Hence net. force is 2F.

13.5 What are the difficulties in testing whether the filament of a lighted bulb obeys Ohm's law?

Electric field lines cannot intersect each other because these are the paths followed by resultant electrostatic force on test charge, which will always be single even if the electrostatic forces are more than one. This is because E has only one direction at any given point. If the lines cross, E would have two directions at a single point i.e. point of intersection of two field lines which is not possible.
